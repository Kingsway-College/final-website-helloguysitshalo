There are different kinds of plates that produce earthquakes.

Such as:

    Convergent boundaries are plates that collide, resulting in subduction (one plate sliding under the other) or mountain building. These boundaries often produce the strongest earthquakes.
    Divergent boundaries are plates that move apart, creating new crust. Earthquakes here are generally less powerful.
    Transform boundaries are plates that slide past each other horizonally. The San Andreas Fault is a famous example of a transform boundry, and it's responsible for many earthquakes in California.
